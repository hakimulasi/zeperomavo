    David Cross Overview and Wiki

    Early Career and Rise to Prominence

    David Cross‘s professional career in television began as a writer on The Ben Stiller Show. This early experience set the stage for his future endeavors in comedy and acting. It was during this time that he met Bob Odenkirk, with whom he would later create the HBO sketch comedy series Mr. Show with Bob and David in 1995.

    This collaboration proved to be a significant milestone in Cross’s career, showcasing his talent in both writing and performing. His work on The Ben Stiller Show earned him an Emmy Award in 1993, highlighting his early success in the industry.

    Breakthrough Role in Arrested Development

    Cross’s career took a major leap with his role as Tobias Fünke in the Fox/Netflix sitcom Arrested Development. This role, originally intended to be minor, became one of his most iconic performances, bringing him widespread recognition and acclaim.

    Diverse Roles in Television, Film, and Voice Acting

    In addition to his work in Arrested Development, Cross has appeared in various television shows and films, demonstrating his range as an actor.

    He had roles in Just Shoot Me!, The Drew Carey Show, NewsRadio, Strangers with Candy, Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job!, and Aqua Teen Hunger Force.

    His voice acting talents were also displayed in roles for Oliver Beene, Curious George, Battle for Terra, the Kung Fu Panda film franchise, Megamind, and Next Gen.

    Cross’s involvement in the Alvin and the Chipmunks film series as Ian Hawke showcased a different aspect of his acting capabilities, appealing to a younger audience.

    Despite the films’ critical reception, they were commercially successful, and Cross’s participation in these projects was a strategic move in his career.

    Stand-Up Comedy and Other Ventures

    David Cross’s stand-up comedy, known for its blend of political commentary and satire, has been a significant part of his career. He has released several comedy albums, including Shut Up You Fucking Baby!, It’s Not Funny, Bigger and Blackerer, Making America Great Again, and Oh, Come On.

    His albums have been nominated for Grammy Awards, reflecting the success and impact of his work in stand-up comedy.

    In addition to his stand-up and acting career, Cross has ventured into directing, with his directorial debut film Hits premiering at the Sundance Film Festival in 2014. He has also authored a book, I Drink for a Reason, which features a mix of memoirs and satirical fictional memoirs.

    Cross’s collaborations extend beyond his work with Bob Odenkirk. He has contributed to various projects, including music videos, magazine columns, and benefit songs.

    Recognition and Influence

    David Cross’s influence in comedy and entertainment is undeniable. He has been recognized for his work with multiple Emmy Award nominations, Grammy Award nominations, and other accolades.

Cross has also been involved in controversies, including a legal issue with Thomas Weber and a public apology to actress-comedian Charlyne Yi for past insensitive remarks.
