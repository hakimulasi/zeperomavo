    Career

    Tommy Haas began playing tennis when he was four years old. He had trained on his own, hitting balls against the wall or into his father’s hands with a wooden plank. In 1996, he became professional and competed in his maiden grand slam in the United States. However, he was defeated in four sets by compatriot Michael Stitch in the first round. He also has four ATP championships under his belt, including his first Master’s trophy in 2001. Similarly, in 2002, he reached the semi-finals by winning in five sets against Todd Martin and Roger Federer, and in four against Marcelo Rios. In his comeback year, 2004, he also won two more ATP titles.

    In 2006, he won three ATP tournaments and advanced to the quarterfinals. He needed to win the Paris Masters to qualify for the ATP year-end finale, the Master’s Cup. In 2007, he was defeated in straight sets by Chilean first-time Grand Slam finalist Fernando González. Similarly, in 2008, he was derailed by injury, missing both the Australian Open and the French Open, which dropped him severely in the rankings. He reached the third round of the US Open in 2009, losing to Fernando Verdasco despite leading by a break in each set. Similarly, he was out of commission between 2010 and 2011 owing to another injury.

    In 2012, he returned to form in the second part of the season, reaching the finals of the German Open Tennis Championships. He won his first championship of the year, defeating Philipp Kohlschreiber in an all-German final, in Munich. In 2014, he began the season in the Heineken Open in Auckland, where he lost in straight sets to Jack Sock in the second round. Then he chose fellow countryman Alexander Waske as his new coach. In 2015, he was upset in a five-setter by Fernando Verdasco in the first round. The next year, he beat Fernando Verdasco in the first round in a five-setter. Similarly, he played his last event at Kitzbühel in 2017. He announced his retirement from the ATP World Tour earlier this year, in 2018.
