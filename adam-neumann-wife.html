    Adam Neumann wife, Rebekah Neumann, played a significant role in WeWork’s rise and fall. 

    As the chief brand and impact officer, Rebekah oversaw WeGrow, the company’s education program.

    She is also known for her endorsement of pseudoscientific beliefs. 

    But because of the dissolution of the business he once oversaw, WeWork co-founder Adam Neumann has been the talk of the town lately. 

    At one point, Neumann’s estimated net worth exceeded $14 billion.

    He was regarded as a visionary businessman who revolutionized the way people worked. 

    But in the end, his hubris and careless actions caused the business he started to fail. 

    Despite from this, Rebekah, Neumann’s wife, has supported him through his highs and lows. Let’s look at the wife of Adam Neumann and her part in the WeWork story.

    Adam Neumann’s wealth survives WeWork’s Bankruptcy

    WeWork founder Adam Neumann remains worth $1.7 billion despite the company’s recent bankruptcy.

     WeWork’s bankruptcy involved $19 billion in liabilities and $15 billion in assets, causing substantial losses for investors like Softbank. 

    Neumann, who left the company in 2019, maintained his wealth.

    While WeWork’s stock plummeted over 99%, he had extracted large sums during better times. 

    He’s now engaged in a new startup called Flow, backed by venture capital firm Andreessen Horowitz. 

    Neumann’s connections to WeWork may continue, as there are discussions about his potential involvement in the business post-bankruptcy.

    Who is Adam Neumann wife?

    Rebekah Neumann is an American businesswoman and entrepreneur. 

    She was born on February 26, 1978, and grew up in Bedford, New York.

    Neumann attended the Horace Mann School before studying business and Buddhism at Cornell University. 

    After college, she worked at Salomon Smith Barney’s Sales and Trading Program, now known as Morgan Stanley Wealth Management. 

    Rebekah later became a certified Jivamukti yoga instructor, receiving her certification from the Omega Institute for Holistic Studies.

    Rebekah Neumann gained fame as the chief brand and impact officer at WeWork, the company founded by her husband. 

    She also oversaw WeGrow, the company’s education program.

    Rebekah was viewed as a vital member of the WeWork leadership team. 

    However, she was criticized for endorsing pseudoscientific beliefs.

    Are Adam and Rebekah Neumann still married?

    After getting married in 2008, Adam Neumann and Rebekah Paltrow—now Rebekah Neumann—are still wed and have been together for a while. 

    Despite difficulties with their company, WeWork, their love and dedication to one another have endured.

    The couple is still supportive and committed to their family despite having six children together.

    Despite any challenges they may have had in their careers, they have a strong marriage and mutual support.

    Their relationship and dedication to their children show that love and family are important to them despite what occurs in their professional lives.

    Why does Adam call Rebekah Rivka?

    As reported by Hathaway, the two actors playing the Neumanns in the WeCrashed podcast even used their characters’ nicknames for each other. 

    “He would call me Rivka,” Hathaway says, “and I would call him more.” (In Hebrew, Rivka is Rebekah, and motek means sweetheart.)

    The couple’s Hebrew nicknames show their connection to their Jewish heritage and faith.

    Adam Neumann children

    Adam and Rebekah Neumann are proud parents to three boys and three girls. 

    The Neumanns are renowned for their family-centered way of life, consistently placing their six children as a top priority while navigating their entrepreneurial path. 

    Their commitment to maintaining a solid and loving family unit is evident in their efforts to ensure that their children remain central in their lives.

    Where is Rebekah Neumann now?

    Since the collapse of WeWork, Rebekah Neumann has been focusing on her new venture, Student of Life for Life (SOLFL). 

    The company is focused on solving global challenges through innovation and technology. 

    Rebekah has also repurchased some of WeGrow’s assets for SOLFL. Additionally, she has been investing in and advising various startups.
