    Early Life & Biography

    Mayer was born and brought up in Wausau, Wisconsin. Her father, Michael Mayer, was an environmental engineer in a water company, and her mother, Margaret Mayer, is an art teacher. She also one younger brother.

    She has been a timid girl in her childhood and teenage years. In her high school, she took ballet and piano lessons that taught her discipline, confidence, and poise. She was good at science and math subject in her school days.

    She attended Wausau West High School, Mayer. She showed excellent performance in academics and extra-curricular activities. She scored well in various subjects such as chemistry, biology, physics, and calculus. In her high school, she became Spanish club President, Key Club treasurer, pom-pom squad, and debate team captain. Shen graduated in 1993, and later chosen by Tommy Thompson, who has been the Governor of Wisconsin. She was selected as a state delegate to participate in the National Youth Science Camp, West Virginia.

    She attended pre-medical classes at Stanford University but changed her major from neuroscience to symbolic systems. She completed her B.S degree in symbolic systems from Stanford. In 1999, she completed her MS in computer science with a specialization in artificial intelligence (AI). She holds various patents in AI and interface design.

    On December 12, 2009, she got married to an investor and lawyer, Zachary Bogue. On September 30, 2012, she delivered a baby boy named Macallister. On December 10, 2015, she gave birth to twin girls, Sylvana, and Marielle.

    Career

    After graduating from Stanford University, Mayer joined Google in 1999. Her work involved writing code and overlooking teams of engineers, and designing, and developing search offerings of Google. Later she got promoted to the post of director of customer web products. She monitored the layout of Google’s search homepage.

    In 2002, Mayer began the (APM) Associate Product Manager program. It was a Google mentorship initiative to hire new talents and promote them for management roles.

    In 2005, she became VP of Search Products and Customer Experience. She performed key roles in Google Images, Google Search, Google Maps, Google News, Google Product Search, Google Books, iGoogle, Gmail, and Google Toolbar. Mayer has served at this post till 2010.

    In 2011, she held Google’s acquisition of Zagat (a survey site) for $125 million. At that time, she was also honored by the Forsythe Award and Centennial Teaching Award.

    In July 2012, she was appointed as the CEO and president of Yahoo. She was a member of the panel of directors of the company.

    On May 20, 2013, she held Yahoo! to buy Tumblr for $1.1 billion.

    On June 13, 2017, she declared resignation from her services.

    On 8 November 2017, she, along with various former and present corporate CEOs, gave statements regarding key security violations at Yahoo in 2013 and 2014.

    Awards & Achievements

    In her long-term and successful career, she has received several awards.

    She was named in the Fortune magazine’s yearly list of US’ Fifty Highly Powerful Business Women in 2008, 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, and 2014 with positions at 50th, 44th, 42nd, 38th, 14th, 8th, and 16th respectively.

    In 2009, Mayer was honored by the Glamour Magazine’s Women of the Year Award.

    She was also ranked in Forbes Magazine’s List of The Top Hundred Powerful Women in The World in 2012, 2013, and 2014, with positions of 20th, 32nd, and 18th, respectively.

    In September 2013, she became the first Chief Executive Officer of a Fortune 500 firm to have got mentioned in a “Vogue magazine.”

    On 24 December 2015, she was ranked at No. 14th by UK-based firm “Richtopia” in the list of five hundred Highly Influential CEOs.
