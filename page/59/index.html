Ohio State's Buckeye Mascot Explained

29 Aug 2024, 00:00

According to Ohio State, the word "Buckeye" was first applied to an Ohioan in 1788, more than a decade before the midwestern area became a state. Native Americans were said to have given Colonel Ebenezer Sproat, a delegate at a Northwest Territory court session, the name of "Hetuck" — their word for "buckeye" — perhaps because he was, at 6'4", nearly as tall as a tree. It was his nickname the rest of his life, and eventually became adopted by all Ohioans.
